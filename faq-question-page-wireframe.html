Is Fresh Dog Food Better Than Kibble?

📖 5 min read
✅ Vet-reviewed
🔄 Updated Jan 2025
Quick Answer
Yes. Fresh dog food is minimally processed, which means nutrients remain intact and more bioavailable for your dog. Studies show dogs on fresh food have better digestion, healthier coats, and improved markers of long-term health compared to kibble-fed dogs.

The Science Behind Fresh vs. Processed Food

The difference between fresh dog food and kibble comes down to one word: processing. And that processing has a significant impact on what your dog actually absorbs from their food.

Kibble is manufactured at extremely high temperatures—often over 400°F—through a process called extrusion. This high heat destroys many of the natural nutrients in the ingredients, which is why synthetic vitamins have to be sprayed back on afterward.

Fresh food, on the other hand, is gently cooked at lower temperatures. This preserves more of the natural vitamins, minerals, and amino acids that dogs need. More importantly, it keeps nutrients in their natural, bioavailable form—meaning your dog's body can actually use them.

What the Research Shows

A yearlong study conducted in partnership with Cornell University tracked dogs fed fresh food versus those on traditional kibble. The results were significant:

30 days
That's all it took for dogs on fresh food to show measurably improved markers of long-term health, including better gut microbiome diversity and reduced inflammation.

The study found that dogs eating fresh food showed:

  • Improved digestibility — Dogs absorbed significantly more nutrients from fresh food
  • Better stool quality — Smaller, firmer stools indicate more efficient digestion
  • Healthier gut bacteria — More diverse and beneficial microbiome populations
  • Reduced inflammation markers — Lower levels of inflammatory compounds in the blood
[Infographic: Fresh Food vs. Kibble Nutrient Comparison]

Why Digestibility Matters

When we talk about dog food, people often focus on the ingredient list. But what matters more is what your dog can actually absorb from those ingredients.

Fresh food is inherently more digestible because the proteins haven't been denatured by extreme heat. When proteins are cooked at very high temperatures, they change structure and become harder for the digestive system to break down.

This is why many dog owners notice immediate changes when switching to fresh food:

  • Less gas and bloating
  • Smaller, less smelly stools
  • Better energy levels
  • Improved coat quality
  • More enthusiasm at mealtime

What About Cost?

Yes, fresh dog food costs more than kibble. But the comparison isn't quite apples to apples.

With kibble, you're paying for convenience and shelf stability. The heavy processing that destroys nutrients is what allows kibble to sit on a shelf for months (or years). You're essentially paying for food that's been engineered to last, not to nourish.

With fresh food, you're paying for actual nutrition. Many customers find that their dogs eat less food overall (because they're absorbing more nutrients) and have fewer vet bills related to digestive issues, allergies, and other diet-related problems.

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Fresh food preserves more natural nutrients than heavily processed kibble
  • Dogs show health improvements within 30 days of switching to fresh food
  • Better digestibility means your dog absorbs more from less food
  • Improved digestion leads to smaller stools, less gas, and better energy
  • Long-term, many pet parents see reduced vet costs from diet-related issues
